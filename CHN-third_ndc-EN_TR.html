China's 2035 Nationally Determined Contribution Report

Climate change is a common challenge facing all mankind, and coping with climate change is a common cause for mankind. China has always attached great importance to dealing with climate change, implemented the national strategy of actively responding to climate change, and effectively promoted the implementation of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change (hereinafter referred to as the Convention) and its Paris Agreement, contributing China's wisdom and strength to global climate governance. In accordance with the requirements of the Paris Agreement and relevant decisions, this time submit the 2035 China Nationally Determined Contribution Report.

I. China's concept of dealing with climate change and implementation results

President Xi Jinping has repeatedly stressed that dealing with climate change is not what others want us to do, but what we do ourselves. It is an inherent requirement of China's sustainable development and a responsibility to promote the construction of a community with a shared future for mankind. As a responsible developing country, China has always kept its promises, adhered to the principle of common but differentiated responsibility, adhered to multilateralism and win-win cooperation, and made important contributions to global climate governance.

(I) China's concept of dealing with climate change

Adhere to the new development concept. China adheres to Xi Jinping's Thought on Ecological Civilization, based on the new stage of development, and upholds the new development concept of innovation, coordination, green, openness and sharing. In the new development concept, green development is a necessary condition for sustainable development and an important embodiment of people's pursuit of a better life. China coordinates industrial restructuring, pollution control, ecological protection and climate change response, and jointly promotes carbon reduction, pollution reduction, green expansion and growth.

Adhere to the system concept. China coordinates the domestic and international situations, handles the relationship between development and emission reduction, overall and partial, short-term and medium- and long-term, integrates the response to climate change into the overall layout of ecological civilization construction and the overall situation of economic and social development, and integrates the system concept into the whole process of carbon peaking and carbon neutrality.

Adhere to green and low-carbon development. China firmly adheres to the path of ecological priority, conservation and intensive green and low-carbon high-quality development. Taking the carbon peak and carbon neutralization work as the guide, it deepens the reform of the ecological civilization system, improves the green and low-carbon development mechanism, accelerates the comprehensive green transformation of economic and social development, and forms a spatial pattern and industrial structure to save resources and protect the environment. , production mode, lifestyle.

Adhere to the people-centered approach. The fundamental interests of the greatest number of people in dealing with climate change are the urgent need to enhance the people's sense of gain, happiness and security in the field of ecological environment. China adheres to the people's first and life first, fully considers the people's yearning for a better life, the expectation of a better environment, and the responsibility to future generations, and actively responds to climate change from the height of responsibility to human civilization.

Adhere to the concerted promotion of carbon reduction, pollution reduction and green growth. China accelerates the comprehensive green transformation of economic and social development, actively responds to climate change, holds the 'bull's nose' of carbon reduction, grasps the integrity of pollution prevention and control and climate governance, focuses on structural adjustment and layout optimization, coordinates to promote environmental benefits, climate benefits, economic benefits and social benefits, and promotes economic Comprehensive green transformation of social development.

Adhere to the same importance as slowing down and adapting. China has always adhered to the importance of mitigation and adaptation, promoted green and low-carbon development, controlled greenhouse gas emissions, and improved carbon sink capacity. At the same time, we should strengthen the impact and risk assessment of climate change in key regions, strengthen the climate resilience of natural ecosystems and economic and social systems, and effectively respond to the adverse effects and risks of climate change.

Adhere to building a community with a shared future for mankind. China adheres to the concept of a community with a shared future for mankind, actively promotes the construction of a fair, reasonable, win-win global climate governance system, strengthens South-South cooperation to deal with climate change, jointly build a green "Belt and Road" and promote global sustainable development. China adheres to multilateralism, adheres to the principle of fairness, common but differentiated responsibilities and respective capabilities, and always advocates that the Paris Agreement represents the general direction of global green and low-carbon transformation, and all countries must take decisive steps.

(II) Progress and results of China's response to climate change

China firmly implements the national strategy to actively respond to climate change. In 2021, it submitted its Nationally Determined Contributions to the secretariat of the Convention by 2030, including: striving to reach the peak of carbon dioxide emissions by 2030 and striving to achieve carbon neutrality by 2060. By 2030, China's unit GDP carbon dioxide emissions will decrease by more than 65% compared with 2005, non-fossil energy will account for about 25% of primary energy consumption, forest storage will increase by 6 billion cubic meters compared with 2005, and the total installed capacity of wind power and solar power generation will reach 12 More than 100 million kilowatts. This is a major strategic decision made by China based on the inherent requirements of promoting responsibility for building a community with a shared future for mankind and achieving sustainable development. As a responsible developing country, China actively contributes to global climate governance, insists on the importance of mitigation and adaptation, and comprehensively promotes the Nationally Determined Contribution and implementation of the country and achieves positive results.

Substantial progress has been made towards the Nationally Determined Contribution target in 2030. Carbon dioxide emissions per unit of GDP continue to decline. According to preliminary calculations, non-fossil energy will account for 19.8% of primary energy consumption in 2024. By the end of 2024, the forest storage capacity will exceed 20 billion cubic meters (an increase of 7 billion cubic meters compared with 2005), and the total installed capacity of wind power and solar power generation will reach 1.41 billion kilowatts, achieving the goal promised to the international community more than six years ahead of schedule.

The green and low-carbon transformation in the energy field has achieved remarkable results. The development of non-fossil energy has accelerated, and the installed scale of new energy has ranked first in the world for many years, accounting for about 40% of the world. Accelerate the construction of large-scale wind power photovoltaic bases and major hydropower projects in the 'Shago Wilderness', and actively develop nuclear power in a safe and orderly manner. Continue to deepen the clean and efficient utilization of fossil energy, accelerate the implementation of the "three reform linkage" of coal-power energy-saving and carbon-reduction transformation, heating transformation and flexibility transformation, and steadily and orderly eliminate the backward production capacity of coal-fired power. Increase the treatment of loose coal. By the end of 2024, coal consumption will be reduced by more than 80 million tons. The clean heating rate in the northern region has reached 83%. A total of more than 40 million households have been completed with "coal-to gas" and "coal-to-electricity". The contribution of clean heating to replace loose coal to the improvement of the regional air environment has reached more than 30%. According to preliminary calculations, the proportion of coal consumption in 2024 was 53.2%, down 14.2 percentage points from 2013. The production and utilization of natural gas have increased steadily. Accelerate the construction of new power systems, promote the construction of water pumping energy storage, new energy storage, flexible adjustment of coal power and other resources, maintain the overall utilization rate of new energy at a high level, and build the world's largest clean power generation system and transmission network. The scale of green electricity transactions has been steadily expanding, and the International Renewable Energy Electricity Consumption Initiative (RE100) unconditionally recognizes China's green certificate.

The green and low-carbon transformation of the industry has taken a solid step. Implement the action plan to improve industrial energy efficiency, and the energy efficiency of cement, electrolytic aluminum, crystalline silicon and other products has reached the world's leading level. Vigorously develop new quality productivity. Since 2020, the output of photovoltaic modules and lithium-ion batteries has increased by 3 times and more than 6 times respectively. Building the world's largest and most complete new energy industry chain, the output of photovoltaic modules has ranked first in the world for 17 consecutive years, providing more than 85% of photovoltaic modules and 60% of wind power equipment to the world. The production and sales of new energy vehicles have ranked first in the world for ten consecutive years. China's new energy industry has enriched global supply, greatly reduced the cost of global energy transformation, and made great contributions to climate change.

Actions to respond to climate change in urban and rural construction and transportation are steadily promoted. Implement energy-saving and carbon-reducing actions in the construction field. In 2024, the new green construction area of urban construction will account for 97.9% of the new urban construction area in that year. The low-carbon transformation of means of transportation equipment has been accelerated. By the end of 2024, the number of new energy vehicles will reach 31.4 million, more than six times that at the end of 2020; the proportion of new energy urban buses account for 82.7% of the total number of public buses and trams, and the annual passenger traffic of urban rail transit is 32.21 billion, accounting for The proportion of total urban passenger traffic has reached 28.3%; the coverage rate of key specialized berths in major ports across the country has exceeded 90%, and key regions, key routes and key ships such as the Yangtze River Economic Belt have basically achieved the normal use of shore electricity; the national railway electrification rate has reached 76.2%; auxiliary power of large and medium-sized airport aircraft The installation rate and utilization rate of device (APU) replacement equipment are more than 95%, and the annual production capacity of sustainable aviation fuel is about 600,000 tons. The green transportation infrastructure continues to be improved. By the end of 2024, a total of 12.818 million charging piles have been built, basically realizing the full coverage of the expressway service area.

The construction of the national carbon market has been accelerated. The national carbon emission rights trading market, which covers the largest greenhouse gas emissions in the world, has been built, and successfully completed five annual performances. According to the Total Quota and Distribution Plan for the National Carbon Emission Trading Power Generation Industry in 2023 and 2024, the key emission units of the power generation industry have been included in the national carbon emission rights trading market. A total of 2,096 houses cover more than 5 billion tons of carbon dioxide emissions annually. In March 2025, the State Council approved the inclusion of the steel, cement and aluminum smelting industries into the national carbon emission rights trading market management. By the end of September 2025, the cumulative turnover of the national carbon emission rights trading market quota was 728 million tons, with a cumulative turnover of 49.83 billion yuan. On January 22, 2024, the National Greenhouse Gas Voluntary Emission Reduction Trading Market was launched, which together with the National Carbon Emission Rights Trading Market, formed China's carbon market system, forming a two-wheel drive pattern. In March 2025, the first batch of certified voluntary emission reduction (CCER) registration will be completed. By the end of September 2025, the cumulative turnover of CCER was 3.184,100 tons, with a cumulative turnover of 267 million yuan, effectively encouraging the development of low-carbon zero-carbon negative carbon technology and becoming an important channel for the value realization mechanism of ecological products. The carbon market has become the main way of carbon pricing in China.

Important progress has been made in the control of non-carbon dioxide greenhouse gases. Issue and implement the Methane Emission Control Action Plan, revise the Coal Seam Gas (Coal Mine Gas) Emission Standards, prohibit the emission limit requirements more strictly, and build a methane monitoring, reporting and verification (MRV) system. Compile and release the Action Plan for Nitrous Oxide Emission Control in the Industrial Sector to clarify the tasks of industrial nitric oxide control in the coming period. Implement the performance requirements of the Kigali Amendment, revise and implement the Regulations on the Administration of Substances that Deplete Ozone, include hydrofluorocarbons in the control scope of substances that deplete the ozone layer, and set the performance target of gradual reduction. Revise and issue the List of Controlled Ozone-Depleting Substances in China, and implement total amount control and quota management for hydrofluorocarbons.

The ability to adapt to climate change is constantly improving. Issued and implemented the National Climate Change Adaptation Strategy 2035, clarifying the goals and tasks of medium- and long-term adaptation to climate change, and issuing local action plans from 30 provinces (regions and cities) and Xinjiang Production and Construction Corps to deepen the pilot construction of 39 climate-adaptable cities. Issued the National Action Plan for Healthy Adaptation to Climate Change (2024-2030). Remarkable results have been achieved in adapting to climate change in key areas. The climate change monitoring, assessment and early warning system have been gradually improved. The China Action Plan for Early Warning to Promote Climate Change Adaptation (2025-2027) has been issued to jointly build a climate adaptation partnership, and the comprehensive disaster prevention and mitigation capacity has been greatly improved. Flood and drought The defense ability is effectively strengthened. The safety resilience of land and space to climate change has been enhanced, the stability of land ecosystems has been significantly improved, the ecological environment of the ocean and coastal zones has been significantly improved, the disaster prevention and mitigation capabilities of farmland and cities have been significantly improved, and remarkable results have been achieved in ecological protection and restoration. The integrated protection and restoration project of landscape, forest, field, lake, grass and sand has been implemented. Since 2016, the protection and restoration area of more than 8.3 million hectares has been completed, the restoration and treatment of more than 350,000 hectares of abandoned mines, about 1,780 kilometers of coastline and about 53,000 hectares of coastal wetlands have been completed. The area of mangroves in China has increased to 30,300 hectares, making it one of the few countries in the world with a net growth in mangrove area. Completed the survey of carbon reserves in 39 typical distribution areas of mangroves, seagrass beds and salt marshes across the country, and initially established the carbon density parameter database of China's blue carbon ecosystem. The climate resilience in the field of health and public health continues to increase, the process of urban adaptation to climate change continues to advance, and a good situation of broad participation of the whole society has initially formed.

The climate governance guarantee system is gradually improved. The Ecological Environment Code (Draft) sets up a special chapter on climate change and promulgates the Energy Law of the People's Republic of China and the Wetland Protection Law of the People's Republic of China. The construction of the carbon peak carbon neutrality '1+N' policy system has been completed and continuously implemented, and the "Opinions on Promoting the Gradual Shift to Dual Control of Energy Consumption to Dual Control of Carbon Emissions", "Work Plan for Accelerating the Construction of a Carbon Emission Dual Control System", "Opinions on Promoting Green Low-carbon Transformation and Strengthening the Construction of the National Carbon Market", "Ecosystem Carbon Implementation Plan for Consolidating and Improving the Capacity of Hui. The Interim Regulations on the Administration of Carbon Emission Rights Trading officially took effect to regulate the transaction of carbon emission rights and related activities. The "Measures for the Administration of Voluntary Greenhouse Gas Emission Reduction Transactions (Trial Implementation)" was issued, and two batches of six methodologies were issued, including afforestation and carbon sink, grid-connected photothermal power generation, grid-connected offshore wind power generation, mangrove creation, coal mine low-concentration gas and wind exhaust gas utilization, and highway lighting energy saving. The "Work Plan for Improving the Carbon Emission Statistical Accounting System" and the "Implementation Plan on the Establishment of a Carbon Footprint Management System" were issued. The carbon emission statistical accounting capacity was gradually improved, the carbon footprint management system was initially built, and the construction of the national greenhouse gas emission factor database began to take shape. Financial, price, investment and financing and other incentive policies and measures have been improved, and the mechanism of climate change and key technology research and development capabilities have been continuously strengthened.

Become a participant, contributor and leader of global climate governance. Actively carry out climate diplomacy between heads of state and inject positive energy into the multilateral climate process. President Xi Jinping has made important speeches on many international occasions to promote the implementation of the Global Development Initiative and put forward the initiative of promoting sovereign equality, observing the international rule of law, practicing multilateralism, and advocating people-oriented and action-oriented global governance initiatives. Initiate the establishment of multilateral consultation mechanisms such as the "Basic Four Countries" and the Ministerial Conference on Climate Action to consolidate political consensus. By September 2025, 54 South-South cooperation documents on climate change have been signed with 42 developing countries to support developing countries to improve their ability to cope with climate change through cooperation in the construction of low-carbon demonstration zones, the gift of climate change mitigation and adaptation materials, and capacity-building training. Fully participate in and guide the climate change process in all fields, and take the initiative to use various multilateral and bilateral mechanisms to deepen the discussion and cooperation on trade and climate issues.

The whole society's awareness of green and low-carbon has been significantly enhanced. Use the National Low Carbon Day and other thematic activities to popularize knowledge of climate change to the public, publicize the results of the work, and continue to carry out the Green Travel Publicity Month and Public Transportation Publicity Week. Improve the degree of public participation in carbon inclusion, promote the construction of ecological environment volunteer service system, and popularize a green low-carbon lifestyle.

II. China's Nationally Determined Contribution target in 2035

In accordance with paragraph 24 of Decision 1/CP.21 of the Conference of the Parties to the Convention, the relevant decisions of the Paris Agreement and the requirements of the resolution of the first global inventory, according to the content of Article 3.1 of the Convention and its own national conditions, development stage, long-term vision and international responsibility, China has determined the national autonomy in 2035 Contribute to the goal.

(I) Overall goal

The goal of China's new round of Nationally Determined Contribution is that by 2035, China's net greenhouse gas emissions across the economy will decrease by 7%-10% compared to the peak, and strive to do better. Non-fossil energy consumption accounts for more than 30% of the total energy consumption, the total installed capacity of wind power and solar power generation has reached more than six times in 2020, striving to reach 3.6 billion kilowatts, forest storage has reached more than 24 billion cubic meters, new energy vehicles have become the mainstream of new sales vehicles, and the national carbon emission rights The trading market covers major high-emission industries, and a climate-adaptable society has been basically built.

(II) Relevant instructions

China's Nationally Determined Contribution submitted herein strictly adheres to the requirements of Article 4, Paragraph 8 of the Paris Agreement and Decision No. 4/CMA.1 of the Conference of the Parties. It covers the information to facilitate clarity, transparency, and understanding of NDCs as set out in Annex I to Decision 4/CMA.1, with the exception of content that is inapplicable to China’s NDC.

The absolute greenhouse gas emission reduction target in China's 2035 Nationally Determined Contribution is economy-wide in scope, covering all greenhouse gases. The year when the net greenhouse gas emissions reach the peak is the benchmark year and the target year is 2035. The value is subject to the data of China's national greenhouse gas inventory.

In 2035, the measurement of China's Nationally Determined Contribution follows the inventory guidelines of the United Nations Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) and the requirements of the Conference of the Parties, and adopts a comprehensive evaluation and scenario analysis research method of combining 'top-down' and 'bow-up' and multi-model comparison, to economic and social development, energy Forecasts of source production and consumption, major industrial production processes, land use, land use changes and forestry, engineering carbon removal, greenhouse gas emission paths, climate change impacts and countermeasures, etc. Activity level data mainly comes from official statistics. The emission factor adopts domestic parameters first, followed by the default value of the IPCC guide.

In 2035, China's Nationally Determined Contribution follows the principles and provisions of the Convention and its Paris Agreement, fully considers the first global inventory and the assessment results in the UAE Consensus, including the global emission gap, the temperature control target path, the collective progress of the parties, etc., and reflects fairness in the way of national independent decision. , the principle of common but differentiated responsibilities and respective capabilities, and taking into account different national conditions, paths and methods, realize the transformation in a just, orderly and fair manner. China actively assumes international obligations in line with its own national conditions, development stage and actual capacity. The target of decreasing net greenhouse gas emissions by 2035 is within the scope of the global inventory of the Paris Agreement and the scenarios of 1.5℃ and 2℃ in the sixth assessment report of the IPCC, which is in line with the net zero carbon resilience to achieve the long-term target of the Paris Agreement. The development path reflects the maximum possible strength and will make outstanding contributions to the implementation of the Convention and its Paris Agreement, the promotion of global energy transformation and the response to the global climate crisis.

China needs to make hard work to achieve the goal of independent national contribution, and it is also inseparable from the international political economic and trade environment and global cooperation conducive to promoting action to deal with climate change. Countries that undermine international cooperation in dealing with climate change and harm the efforts of all parties, including China, to deal with climate change, need to be held accountable.

III. China implements the policies and actions of the country's Nationally Determined Contribution

In order to fully implement the Nationally Determined Contribution of the country in 2035, China has put forward policies and actions around the fields of innovation in the legal system to deal with climate change, greenhouse gas control, adaptation to climate change, and strengthening support and security.

(I) Improve the legal system to deal with climate change

Improve the legal system of climate change. Research and formulate special laws and regulations related to climate change and carbon neutrality. Accelerate the revision of the Code of Ecology and Environment and the Energy Conservation Law of the People's Republic of China, the Electricity Law of the People's Republic of China, the Coal Law of the People's Republic of China, the Renewable Energy Law of the People's Republic of China, the Circular Economy Promotion Law of the People's Republic of China and other laws and Continue to improve the effectiveness of carbon emission management, supervision and law enforcement, and improve the connection mechanism between administrative law enforcement and criminal justice. Strengthen supervision and law enforcement, strengthen judicial protection in the field of climate change response, and improve the climate public welfare litigation system.

Establish and continuously improve the dual control of total carbon emissions and intensity. Integrate carbon emission indicators and related requirements into the national plan, establish and improve local carbon assessment, industry carbon control, enterprise carbon management, project carbon evaluation, product carbon footprint and other policy systems and management mechanisms, and effectively connect with the national carbon emission rights trading market. During the 15th Five-Year Plan, we will implement the double control of carbon emissions with intensity control as the main focus and total volume control as the supplement, and establish a comprehensive evaluation and assessment system for carbon peaking and carbon neutrality. After the carbon peak, the double control of carbon emissions, mainly controlling the total amount and supplemented by intensity control, will be implemented.

Strengthen the construction of the national carbon market. Expand the industry coverage of the carbon emission rights trading market in an orderly manner, steadily implement the combined carbon quota distribution mode of free and paid, increase the proportion of paid distribution in an orderly manner, and enrich the trading varieties and methods. Continue to improve the relevant system of the national greenhouse gas voluntary emission reduction trading market, and continue to develop the methodology of greenhouse gas voluntary emission reduction projects with ecological benefits, economic benefits, social benefits, etc. By 2035, build a more effective, dynamic and internationally influential carbon market.

Strengthen the promotion and application of green science and technology innovation and advanced green technology, strengthen the green low-carbon manufacturing industry, develop the green low-carbon service industry, and build a green low-carbon industrial chain and supply chain. By 2035, the endoc motivation of green development will be significantly enhanced, the scale of green industry will reach a new level, the energy resource utilization efficiency of key industries and key products will reach the international advanced level, and green development will become a common form of new industrialization.

Electricity, promote the large-scale development and utilization of marine energy, develop biomass energy, geothermal energy, etc. according to local conditions, promote the integrated development of water and scenery, and promote the development of the whole chain of hydrogen energy 'production, storage and transmission'. Develop an energy-use industry system that adapts to the characteristics of new energy. Coordinate planning to guide the green and low-carbon transformation of urban and rural construction. Annex 1

Policies and actions of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region to deal with climate change

The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region (hereinafter referred to as the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region) has established carbon reduction strategies and measures through the Hong Kong Climate Action Blueprint 2050 (hereinafter referred to as the Blueprint), and strives to halve carbon emissions from the level of 2005 by 2035 and achieve carbon neutrality by 2050.

The total greenhouse gas emissions of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region has peaked in 2014; the total emissions in 2023 have decreased by about 19% and 24% respectively compared with the peak in 2005 and 2014; the per capita emissions are about 4.58 tons of carbon dioxide equivalent, down nearly 30% over 2014.

I. Policies, actions and goals to deal with climate change

(I) Institutional arrangements

The Chief Executive of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region presided over the Climate Change and Carbon Neutrality Supervision Committee to formulate overall strategies and supervise the coordination of various actions. The Office of Climate Change and Carbon Neutrality of the Environment and Ecology Bureau of the Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region coordinates the in-depth carbon reduction and adaptation to climate change of the Policy Bureau and departments, and encourages all sectors of society to actively participate in climate action.

(II) Reduce greenhouse gas emissions

The Blueprint puts forward the following four major carbon reduction strategies to lead the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region towards carbon neutrality.

1. Net zero power generation

The goal is to ban coal-fired for daily power generation by 2035 or before, switch to natural gas and zero-carbon energy, and strive to increase the proportion of zero-carbon energy in the power generation fuel portfolio to 60%-70%, so as to achieve the goal of "net zero power generation" by 2050.

The Hong Kong SAR government has taken the lead in developing renewable energy and introduced measures to support the private sector to develop renewable energy. It is expected to increase the proportion of solar energy in the power generation fuel portfolio from about 0.5% in 2022 to about 1% in 2028.

2. Energy-saving and green construction

More than 50% of carbon emissions come from electricity production related to building energy consumption. The goal of the Hong Kong SAR government is to reduce the electricity consumption of commercial buildings by 30%-40% by 2050 or by 2050, reduce the electricity consumption of residential buildings by 20%-30%, and achieve half of the above two targets by 2035 or before 2035.

The Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region has revised the Building Energy Efficiency Ordinance to supervise more categories of buildings, enforce the disclosure of energy audit report data and shorten the energy audit cycle; and continue to update the Building Energy Efficiency Code and Energy Audit Code under the Ordinance every three years.

The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region has implemented the mandatory energy efficiency labeling program to let consumers know the energy efficiency performance of the 11 categories of specified products covered by the plan. The label plan is estimated to save about 270 million degrees of electricity per year after the third rating standard upgrade in 2025.

3. Green transportation

The goal is to achieve zero carbon emissions in the transportation sector by 2050. The Roadmap for the Popularization of Electric Vehicles in Hong Kong proposes to stop the registration of new fuel and hybrid private cars in 2035 or before. The number of electric vehicles has increased from about 180 in 2010 to about 110,000 in 2024; the proportion of electric private cars to newly registered private cars jumped from 6.3% in 2019 to About 71% in 2024; the goal is to increase the number of public and private charging parking spaces to about 200,000 by mid-2027.

The cross-departmental working group on hydrogen energy established by the Hong Kong SAR Government promotes the local application of hydrogen energy through pilot projects. As of October 2025, the applications for 28 hydrogen energy pilot projects have been approved in principle, including hydrogen buses, hydrogen energy street washing cars and the first public hydrogen filling station. The Hong Kong SAR Government announced the Hong Kong Hydrogen Energy Development Strategy in mid-2024, putting forward four strategies of "improving regulations", "formulating standards", "coordinating with the market" and "prudently promoting", so as to create an environment conducive to the development of local hydrogen energy in a steady and orderly manner, and also expand cooperation between the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region and the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area and even the world. Integrate into the overall situation of national development and develop new quality productive forces.

The Hong Kong SAR Government has amended the legislation in July 2025 to establish a framework for regulating hydrogen fuel, and will prepare a certification model for docking with international hydrogen energy standards in 2027 or before.

The Hong Kong Airport Authority and major aviation-related business partners have made a commitment to achieve net zero carbon emissions by the end of 2050, and have formulated a carbon management action plan, including the electrification of vehicles in the restricted area of the airport, the addition of electric vehicle charging stations, and the trial use of hydrogen energy. In order to promote the development of the new energy industry in the aviation industry, the Hong Kong SAR government will consult with the mainland government to lead an enterprise of a major global sustainable aviation fuel (SAF) supplier to develop in the Greater Bay Area in order to achieve the target of 1% to 2% of SAF use for flights departing from Hong Kong International Airport in 2030.

As the first port in Asia to force ocean-going ships to moor and convert to low-sulfur fuel oil, Hong Kong, China and mainland China have worked together to reduce ship pollutant emissions, including the establishment of ship emission control zones in the waters of the Pearl River Delta with the Guangdong Provincial Government. The Green Marine Fuel Refueling Action Program will be announced in 2024 to develop Hong Kong into a high-quality green marine fuel refueling center.

4. National waste reduction

The goal is to get rid of dependence on landfills to treat household waste by 2035 or before, and move towards zero waste landfill through national waste reduction, resource recycling and development of sufficient waste to energy facilities. The amount of urban solid waste disposal will continue to decline from 2021.

The legislation on the control of disposable tape tableware and other plastic products has been implemented in April 2024, and a legal framework for producer responsibility schemes applicable to different products will also be established.

II. Improve the ability to adapt to climate change and adapt to change

The main measures in adapting to climate change and responding capacity include:

(I) Strengthen infrastructure

The "Climate Change Infrastructure Working Group" will update the design standards of infrastructure in a timely manner according to the latest parameters, coordinate relevant research, and formulate measures and implementation plans to improve the ability to defend against extreme weather.

(II) Respond to sea level rise and protect the coast

Formulate and promote improvement engineering and management measures, as well as strategies and guidelines for long-term defense measures and management for identified coastal areas. The Hong Kong Observatory provides the sea level climate estimate of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region on the latest assessment of the United Nations Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC).

(3) Respond to extreme rainstorms and tropical cyclones

The Hong Kong Observatory continues to strengthen the meteorological observation network to support extreme weather monitoring. The Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region has a storm surge early warning system and a sub-district flooding risk assessment system to support emergency departments in preparing for and implementing contingency plans, and issuing early warnings to the public.

Continuously assess the risk of flooding, formulate flood control management strategies and carry out rainwater drainage system improvement projects. In order to cope with the risk of mudslides, we will continue to implement the Long-term Plan for the Prevention and Control of Mudslides, consolidate the government's artificial slopes and carry out risk mitigation projects for natural hillsides.

(4) Respond to extreme drought and ensure water supply

Implement the "comprehensive water resources management strategy" to improve the adaptability of water supply by promoting water conservation, strengthening water loss management measures, expanding the use of secondary water, and building seawater desalination plants.

(5) Dealing with the hot weather

Promote green building design and promote urban forestry to moderate rising temperatures, and examine the design standards of infrastructure and buildings to cope with extreme temperatures.

The Hong Kong Observatory launched "continuous high temperature" and "extreme heat" tips to remind the public to take precautions. The Labor Department launched the "Guidelines for the Prevention of Heatstroke at Work" and formulated the "Working Heat Warning" to remind employers and employees to take preventive measures according to the warning level.

(6) Strengthen the response to natural disasters

The Natural Disaster Response Plan describes the strategy, organizational structure, functions and responsibilities of the unit, the warning system of the Hong Kong SAR Government in response to natural disasters, and the coordination and response of the "Emergency Monitoring and Support Center" when necessary. In the event of a large-scale natural disaster, the Secretary of the Political Affairs Department will lead the supervision committee to provide high-level coordination and supervision, and set priority for emergency, so that the society can return to normal as soon as possible. Guangdong, Hong Kong and Macao have implemented the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area Emergency Rescue Action Plan, built an emergency action mechanism, and improved the regional disaster disposal capacity.

III. Relevant research and safeguards

The Hong Kong SAR Government has established the 'Low-Carbon Green Scientific Research Fund' and invested a total of 400 million Hong Kong dollars. A total amount of about 147 million Hong Kong dollars has been approved to fund 33 scientific research projects to reduce carbon and strengthen environmental protection.

The green and sustainable financial market of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region is booming. The total amount of related debt issued in Hong Kong in 2024 exceeded 84 billion US dollars, of which the total amount of green and sustainable bonds arranged for issuance in Hong Kong is about 43 billion US dollars, accounting for about 45% of the total amount in Asia, ranking first in the Asian market for seven consecutive years since 2018. The Hong Kong SAR Government has successfully issued government green bonds equivalent to about $31 billion. CoreClimate provides Hong Kong dollar and RMB settlement for international voluntary carbon credit product transactions. The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region will continue to develop green and sustainable finance to meet the financing needs of the world's green transformation.

IV. International and regional cooperation

The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region continues to participate in international alliances related to climate change, including the Urban Climate Leadership Alliance (C40), the Global Climate Energy Mayor's Covenant and the United Nations World Meteorological Organization. The Hong Kong Special Administrative Region continues to promote regional cooperation between Guangdong and Hong Kong through the "Guangdong-Hong Kong Cooperation Group on Environmental Protection and Climate Change Response" and the "Special Group on Climate Change Response". Guangdong, Hong Kong and Macao jointly compile the "Communical on Climate Monitoring of the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area" every year to record the climate situation in the region. The Hong Kong SAR Government provides Hong Kong data and relevant research materials required for the risk assessment of sea level rise in the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area of the Ministry of Natural Resources.

V. Further strengthen policies and actions

In accordance with the spirit of the Paris Agreement, the Hong Kong SAR Government will continue to promote the five-year inspection, adjust and optimize carbon reduction measures, and continue to actively participate in global and regional climate action.

Annex 2

Policies and actions to deal with climate change in the Macao Special Administrative Region

I. Macao's goals and progress in dealing with climate change

In order to achieve the goals of the Paris Agreement and cooperate with the national action goals to deal with climate change, the Macao Special Administrative Region Government (hereinafter referred to as the SAR Government) has always actively promoted the work of responding to climate change. In 2021, the SAR government put forward the target of responding to climate change by 2030: the carbon intensity in 2030 will be 60%-65% lower than in 2005. In the Second Five-Year Plan for Economic and Social Development of the Macao Special Administrative Region (2021-2025), we will further set a more than 55% decrease in carbon intensity in 2025 compared with 2005, and strive to achieve the goal of carbon peaking in 2030 or before. In 2023, Macau's carbon intensity will decrease by 65% compared with 2005.

II. Macao's independent contribution target in 2035

(I) The mitigation target includes

1. Carbon dioxide emissions reach peak

Target type and description. Achieving carbon peaking by 2030 mainly refers to striving for a relatively stable platform period before 2030 or a steady decline after carbon dioxide emissions reach the peak.

Target year and reference year. The benchmark year is not involved, and the target year is 2030.

Caliber and range. The peak of carbon dioxide emissions is energy combustion and carbon dioxide emissions used in industrial production processes and products. Macau does not involve carbon dioxide emissions used in industrial production processes and products. Therefore, the peak of carbon dioxide emissions in Macao is mainly carbon dioxide emissions caused by energy combustion.

2. The total amount of greenhouse gas emissions decreased

Target type and description. Strive for a steady decline in greenhouse gas emissions by 2035, more than 5% lower than in 2030.

Target year and reference year. The benchmark year is 2030, and the target year is 2035, which is a single-year target, with a time span of 5 years.

Caliber and range. The target caliber for the decline in total greenhouse gas emissions is the indirect carbon emissions of energy combustion, waste management and outsourced electricity. The types of gases are carbon dioxide, methane and nitrous oxide.

3. The proportion of non-fossil electricity purchased from outside

Target type and description. In 2035, the proportion of non-fossil power purchased from the southern power grid in Macao will remain at about 55%, among which non-fossil power mainly includes nuclear power, wind power, photovoltaics, hydropower, biomass energy, etc.

Target year and reference year. The target does not involve the benchmark year, and the target year is 2035. Caliber and range. It does not involve the coverage areas and gas types related to greenhouse gas emissions.

4. New car penetration rate of zero-emission vehicles

Target type and description. The proportion of newly registered light vehicles and motorcycles with zero emissions vehicles (electric vehicles or other technologies) reaches 100%, which means that by 2035, the penetration rate of newly registered light vehicles and motorcycles will reach 100%. Light cars mainly refer to cars that do not include the driver's passenger capacity of no more than eight seats or a total weight of no more than 3,500 kilograms. Motorcycles are mainly two-wheeled or three-wheeled vehicles with engines.

Target year and reference year. The benchmark year is not involved, and the target year is 2035.

Caliber and range. It does not involve the coverage areas and gas types related to greenhouse gas emissions.

Five. The intensity of electricity consumption of public buildings

Target type and description. The electricity consumption per unit area of public buildings will be reduced by 5%, which means that the electricity consumption per unit area of public buildings will be reduced by 5% compared with 2025 by 2035. The electricity consumption per unit area is the electricity consumption of public buildings divided by the area of public buildings.

Target year and reference year. The target benchmark year is 2025, and the target year is 2035. It is a single-year target with a time span of 10 years.

Caliber and range. It does not involve the coverage areas and gas types related to greenhouse gas emissions.

(II) Adaptation goals include

1. The field of meteorological monitoring

Build a modern meteorological business, service, scientific and technological innovation and management system with the world's advanced level, and improve the ability of climate change monitoring and early warning.

2. Watershed/Aquide ecosystem

Improve the ability to ensure the safety of water supply, build local storage facilities, and increase the water storage capacity of local water storage facilities to about 3.12 million cubic meters.

3. The field of disaster prevention and mitigation

The main disaster prevention and mitigation projects have been basically completed, the vision of a safe and resilient city has been basically realized, and the government-led and the whole society has participated, covering the whole disaster, whole process and all-round emergency management system.

III. Macao implements the key tasks of national independent contribution

(I) Coordinate and promote greenhouse gas emission control actions

1. Build a low-carbon power system

It is required that the photovoltaic power generation system or vegetation area of the rooftop of newly built public housing should not be less than 30% of the open-air area, and the proportion of renewable energy used in Macao should be gradually increased.

2. Green transformation of land transportation

Formulate the "Macau Electric Vehicle Promotion Plan", study the new registration year of all kinds of fuel vehicles, and launch the "Subsidy Plan for Eliminating Old Motorcycles and Replacing New Electric Motorcycles". Add charging infrastructure in eligible parking lots and public buildings.

3. Building energy saving and carbon reduction

Formulate guidelines for green buildings in Macao for reference by the public sector and the industry. Study the pilot plan for energy-saving transformation of existing buildings, improve the energy efficiency of buildings, and encourage public and private buildings to obtain green building certification. Implement a quota system for the import of hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs) with high global warming potential, and promote the use of refrigerants and fire extinguishing equipment with low global warming potential in new buildings.

4. Reduce waste at the source and turn waste into energy

Implement the polluter's out-of-payment and producer's responsibility plan for all kinds of waste in stages; in 2027, the organic resource recycling center will be put into use to treat kitchen waste and use it for power generation.

(II) Actively adapt to climate change

1. Strengthen the ability of climate change monitoring, early warning and risk assessment

Combined with the "Meteorological Development Plan for the Guangdong-Hong Kong-Macao Greater Bay Area (2020-2035)", the meteorological information industry has developed healthily, and the development level of intelligent meteorological information, the ability of meteorological scientific and technological innovation, and the construction capacity of meteorological services 'Belt and Road' have reached the world's leading level.

2. Enhance the climate resilience of natural ecology and economic and social systems

Improve the water supply system and establish an emergency guarantee mechanism for water supply; strengthen the monitoring of ecosystems and ecological protection and restoration in extreme events, and improve the environmental management of the sea area. Improve the flood control standards of urban infrastructure, and improve the emergency plan for monitoring and prevention and control of extreme events.

3. Strengthen disaster reduction and post-disaster reconstruction actions

In conjunction with the Ten-Year Plan for Disaster Prevention and Mitigation of the Macao Special Administrative Region (2019-2028), we will strengthen the capacity building of disaster prevention and disaster resistance in Macao's flood prevention and drainage, water supply, power supply, gas supply, communication, transportation, housing and other facilities and historical and cultural heritage.

(III) Support and guarantee system

1. Strengthen technological innovation

Study the feasibility of applying carbon capture and sequestration (CCS) technology to generator sets and the application prospects of hydrogen energy in land transportation and power generation. Encourage large enterprises or units with conditions to actively explore and apply low-carbon technology or green technology.

2. Improve social participation

Promote energy conservation and emission reduction in all walks of life through the Macau Environmental Protection Hotel Award and the Green School Partner Program; encourage large enterprises to conduct carbon audits and formulate carbon neutral plans. Integrate low-carbon information into various activities, strengthen publicity and promotion, and guide the public to fulfill their obligations of energy conservation and emission reduction.

3. Actively promote international cooperation in dealing with climate change

Macau continues to play the role of a window platform to connect China and Portuguese-speaking countries, and promote cooperation with meteorological departments of Portuguese-speaking countries in numerical weather forecasting, disaster weather warning and public meteorological services, so as to jointly promote the development of meteorological technology and services. Actively rely on existing resources such as the Secretariat of the UN ESCAP/World Meteorological Organization's Typhoon Commission to deepen international cooperation in the field of early warning and assessment of climate change.
