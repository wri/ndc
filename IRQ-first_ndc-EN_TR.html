Nationally Determined Contributions of Iraq (NDC)

Executive Summary

This document is a voluntary national contribution and a vision that represents Iraq's supreme policy in dealing with the problem of climate change nationally and internationally.

Iraq, as a developing country, believes that solutions to the problem of climate change will result only with the active participation of all countries and within the principles of the United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change and the Paris Agreement, which are built on the basis of common but differentiated responsibility and according to circumstances, national capabilities and historical responsibility in the occurrence of this problem.

From an early age to accession to the agreement, Iraq began working on the implementation of many projects and studies aimed at adapting to the effects of climate change and mitigating emissions, but most of these projects have stopped completely as a result of the fierce confrontation with the terrorist gangs of ISIS, which caused catastrophic results that affected all sectors life and threatened security and community peace, complete destruction of infrastructure, and displacement of hundreds of thousands of people, in addition to the damage to the economic sector, which was already affected by fluctuation and low oil prices, the terrorist organization’s control of a number of oil wells and oil and industrial facilities, and Iraq’s compelling to allocate a large part of its financial budget to finance operations This is a real challenge that has contributed to draining the country’s economic and human energies in the face of terrorism, and this situation has exacerbated the conditions of the Corona pandemic that it facedThe country, like the rest of the world, since the beginning of 2020 until now, has greatly affected the health reality, which is already suffering from fragility, weakness and lack of cadres of all kinds, in addition to the cracking of infrastructure as a result of the confrontations that the country has gone through, which led to an increase in the level of unemployment and poverty.

The impact of climate change on Iraq has been clear in the significant decrease in water resources in it and an increasing increase in temperature rates that exceed global rates, however, Iraq presents this ambitious plan in which all national plans and strategies related to climate change have been adopted to be compatible with needs and trends National development for all sectors to ensure the achievement of the desired goals nationally and internationally through qualitative and radical gradual transformations and according to the national capabilities available in the event that security and peace are achieved and economic resources are provided to ensure the achievement of a decent life for the people of Iraq.

Chapter One

National conditions and capabilities :

Iraq is located in southwest Asia within the dry and semi-arid regions. It has acquired from this site its semi-continental climate, influenced by the Mediterranean climate. The climate of Iraq is characterized by a wide daily and annual thermal range, due to the lack of large water bodies that reduce the coldness of winter and heat of summer, and the lack of rain In the direction from the northeast to the southwest, and due to its geographical location, it is considered one of the countries most vulnerable to the effects of global warming and climatic changes in its multiple dimensions .

However, despite this, Iraq supports the global trend and the paths set by the Paris Agreement, which aims to preserve the planet and calls on the developed and major industrial countries to abide by the promises of mitigation, provide financial and technical support, transfer technology and implement transparently towards developing countries to help them implement their national contributions and achieve development goals sustainable development within its lands and enabling them to withstand through the establishment of a diversified, flexible and responsive economy to current and future climate changes. Therefore, Iraq’s desire is strong towards the reconstruction of destroyed cities according to modern building regulations to become sustainable green cities that are environmentally friendly, so that they serve as living evidence of peace within the Iraqi society to encourage the use of renewable energy and green construction To ensure sustainability in the face of climate change risks, especially after the conditions of the Corona pandemic, which worsened the situation, as the future economic prospects for Iraq involve great challenges; Where it is expected to lead to a collapse in prices

Iraq has natural ecosystems rich in biodiversity, but its geographical location within the arid and semi-arid areas made it one of the countries most vulnerable to the impact of global climate change, and this is evidenced by climatic phenomena that were not known before, such as low rates of rain, low levels of lakes and rivers, and high temperatures at rates Unprecedented by more than double the global average, the occurrence of fires, the increase in the intensity and frequency of dust storms and the duration of their occurrence, and the increase in the phenomenon of desertification, which caused pressure on the ecosystems and the balances they cause, which contributed to reducing the spread of many types of endemic plants and the decrease or extinction of wild animals as a result of The impact of climate change, according to the International Union for Conservation of Nature ( IUCN ).This is considered a direct threat in terms of severity to ecosystems and biodiversity, but despite these pressures, large areas of Iraq are still of global and local importance, for example, the marshes areas, which were included on the World Heritage List for their importance, in addition to declaring many protected areas with Natural richness and the environmental services it provides with an important economic return.

Surface water is the main water resource in Iraq and consists of the waters of the Tigris and Euphrates rivers and their tributaries and the Shatt al-Arab. There has been a significant decrease in Iraq’s water resources due to the many dams and irrigation and development projects that were established on these rivers in neighboring countries to secure their water shares and use them to generate hydroelectric power, which affected Iraq’s water share and quality, and the effects of climatic changes have caused a decrease in the quantities of water entering its rivers, The water sector is considered one of the most vulnerable sectors in the face of climate change.

The issue of the rise in the level, acidity and temperature of sea water is of great importance to Iraq, although the coast of our country on the Arabian Gulf is very narrow, but the rise in sea level could threaten the southern region with drowning, especially the province of Basra, which overlooks the Gulf, in which the economic power of Iraq is concentrated. In addition, the increase in temperature and acidity of these waters affects the biodiversity in the marine environment and coral reefs in the territorial waters of Iraq and also affects the tourism aspect in this area. Therefore, this topic is of exceptional importance in dealing with the file of climate change and its effects. Support the international community to identify the means to address this.

The agricultural sector is one of the most water consuming sectors in Iraq (about 35 billion cubic meters/year), where the water consumption rate in this sector constitutes 85%, due to the use of open and old irrigation channels, the fragility or weakness of the management of water resources and dilapidated infrastructure, and the use of techniques Old irrigation (surface irrigation) for some crops such as rice and traditional farming patterns. The quantities of irrigation water exceeded the specified water quotas, which, over time, led to a rise in water levels in them, waterlogging and salinization of the soil .

The transport sector is one of the important sectors in the economy of any country. In addition, all economic and life activities and activities such as shopping, housing and entertainment cannot perform their role and function without linking to transport lines because of the impact of other economic sectors such as industry, trade and tourism, and this sector suffers from deteriorating structures. Infrastructure, which is the mainstay for the development of transportation, due to the lack of laws, legislation and projects that support the trend towards sustainable, collective and environmentally friendly transportation.

Iraq suffers from a severe weakness in the infrastructure supporting the industrial sector, due to the lack of cities and industrial areas that have the necessary infrastructure to establish various industries, in addition to the weak infrastructure supporting industry and investment, which contributed to the deterioration of this sector. The weakness of the comprehensive knowledge infrastructure for communications and information technology networks, as well as the infrastructure for standards, metrology and quality, with the weakness of efforts made to support the private sector to become a key partner in economic development through clear and developed programs, and all this clearly and tangibly affected the reality of the industrial sector and its inability to keep pace with the march global in progress.

Environmental laws and regulations:

The Iraqi state, especially after 2003, issued many legislations aimed at protecting the environment, but the country's security conditions did not help in their full implementation, and there is a need for other legislation and updating the existing ones to cope with the requirements of facing climate changes.

Environmental Awareness:

Environmental awareness is a generalization and deepening of environmental knowledge among all segments and groups of society through appropriate environmental means and messages for each of them and motivating them to contribute and participate individually and collectively in protecting the environment and preserving its resources. Given that climate change is the problem of the times and that women and children are more vulnerable than men to the problem of climate change, which It requires full environmental awareness towards climate changes and the development of strategies, plans, laws and legislations for environmental awareness among all segments of society, especially the vulnerable groups.

research and development:

Addressing climate change requires strengthening scientific research and technological development in several areas, including technology transfer, economic aspects, industry, oil, energy, transportation development, and other areas related to climate change and citizen's life in order to mitigate the effects of climate change and adapt to it. Giving importance to this aspect and providing the opportunity to prepare extensive and in-depth studies on climate change and its multifaceted effects and support graduate students (Masters and Ph. The Iraqi environmental, economic and social reality.

Chapter Two

Climate change impacts, fragility and adaptation measures

The effects of climate change on the various Iraqi sectors have become known to all and have cast a shadow over life in Iraq, especially in light of the fragile conditions of the country and dilapidated infrastructure. Economic, health and life in general, where projections based on national numerical climate models indicate a steady increase in temperature ranging from (0.9 ° C) since 2007, it may reach (3.5 ° C) in the year 2100 according to the aforementioned forecasts, which increases From the critical situation in a country where the temperature has risen above (50 ° C)) in a number of summer days of the year, not to mention the obvious decrease in the annual rates of precipitation, which are expected to witness a significant and continuous decrease, which may reach in the year 2100 to a decrease of more than 30% from its rates during the period 1938-1978 according to the forecasts of the authority The general weather forecast and Iraqi seismic monitoring, and this is a clear indication of the reasons for the clear increase in the occurrence of extreme cases such as heat waves, droughts, and heavy rainfall. Suddenly, there are, for example, large waves of drought that led to an increase in desertification, which affected the increase in dust and dust storms During the year, the intensity, frequency, recurrence and duration of it led to a noticeable increase in the number of asthma cases in the Iraqi society, especially among children and the elderly, and the spread of diseases and cancers significantly in recent years. In addition, climate change has exacerbated the problem of water scarcity in the Tigris and Euphrates rivers and their tributaries. As indicated by the bank's estimatesThe 2011 International Convention on the existence of a significant shortage and scarcity of renewable water resources for Iraq compared to the actual need during the period from 2000 to 2009, while this percentage is expected to reach 37% during the period from 2020 to 2030, which will increase to 51% during the period from 2040 Until 2050, this explains the reason for the great collapse in the agricultural sector, which left its clear effects on agricultural production, the economy of Iraq, natural systems, biodiversity, loss of vegetation cover, and the threat to coastal marine areas overlooking the Arabian Gulf, especially in Basra Governorate in southern Iraq, due to sea level rise and warming. Water and the impact on coral reefs and biodiversity in the marine environment and the loss of coastal lands, in addition to its effects on the energy sector, because Basra Governorate is the main center for oil wealth in Iraq, which created pressure on the national economy and a threat to the stability of the country and made itIt is very difficult to achieve sustainable development in all its vital sectorsThis hinders our ambitions in sustainable development and economic development , which calls for rapid intervention and finding solutions based on nature to save these natural systems after they were and still are the main contributors to the stability of the climate of Iraq and the world, especially in the last ten thousand years. One of the five most vulnerable countries in the world to climate change, according to the sixth report of the State of the Global Environment Outlook for Western Asia ( GEO-6).

Iraq is preparing a National Adaptation Plan ( NAP) with funding from the Green Climate Fund ( GCF) in cooperation with the United Nations Environment Program (UNEP)Which will be among the basic plans that the country will rely on in implementing this policy to ensure flexibility for all priority fragile national sectors during the next three years to reduce potential risks and find effective means to sustain water sources and address acute water scarcity and the deterioration of its quality. As a result of the difficult economic conditions that Iraq is going through, especially after the Corona pandemic and the accompanying unprecedented drop in oil prices, its implementation of this policy will require regional cooperation and international support to enable it to establish strong health systems that are resilient and resistant to difficult conditions to protect human health, protect natural systems and biodiversity and enhance Resilience to the impacts and disaster risks associated with local climate change scenarios and enhance adaptation in fragile sectors.

In view of the synergistic relationship between sectors, the preparation and implementation of an action plan for adaptation measures and the achievement of the required benefits for each of the main sectors affected by climate change and within the framework of national and sectoral strategies related to them, such as the national water strategy, the national strategy for agriculture, the national biodiversity strategy and the national framework for integrated management of risks Drought in Iraq, will lead to strengthening the national capacity to face the effects of climate change on different sectors, especially those sectors that are most sensitive and vulnerable to being affected, as follows:

1. Water Resources Sector:

The most important topics:

  1. The sustainable investment of groundwater and its preservation for future generations, water harvesting and reducing water losses, and the use of modern technologies in monitoring and controlling groundwater aquifers and determining their quality, with the need to maintain the hydrogen balance of groundwater in general.

  2. Desalination of sea water (and river water when required) and the use of renewable energy to supply desalination plants with alternative energy and the development of new technologies for that.

  3. Establishing many dams and underground reservoirs to collect water, ward off flood risks, provide the necessary water quotas during droughts, and recharge and sustain the subterranean layers carrying groundwater.

  4. Raising the efficiency of irrigation water use and working to find modern, high-efficiency irrigation methods, reducing water consumption in line with the quality of crops, the nature of the soil and climatic conditions in order to adapt to the sharp decrease in water resources, rehabilitating the main irrigation projects and gradually linking the drains to the main drainage areas or evaporation basins To avoid returning it to the rivers and using non-traditional water sources to provide the necessary water quotas for agricultural, industrial and service uses, such as injecting oil wells, irrigating green belts, and reducing water wastage in quantity and quality.

  5. Activating national policies, laws and legislations that urge citizens to rationalize water use, with the need for certain procedures to be in place against those who deliberately waste water.

  6. Urging the riparian countries on the Tigris and Euphrates rivers and their tributaries (Turkey and Iran) to take into account Iraq’s rights to its common waters and to strengthen regional cooperation with them to achieve water and climate security, which requires assessment of regional risks, joint management of crises, sharing of damage and taking into account the country’s water demands from shared rivers to meet its water needs for development sectors To achieve security and peace.

The overall goal: Increasing the flexibility of the water resources sector in the country by adopting an integrated approach that faces the future increasing demand for water and reducing the potential deficit through developing water uses in line with the challenges of climate change and applying the axes mentioned within the strategy of water and land resources until 2035 by providing support Financial and technical, and the introduction of alternative and non-conventional sources, taking into consideration the improvement of infrastructure, the efficiency of supplies, and the preservation of water sources from pollution as part of the sustainability of these sources.

2. Agriculture sector:

The most important themes:

  1. Reducing and rehabilitating soil degradation, pastures and vegetation cover, improving land management practices, and adopting smart and conservative agriculture, especially in the field of agriculture and forestry.

  2. Modernizing, improving and developing agricultural practices and developing livestock in order to achieve the principles of adaptation to face climate changes.

  3. Establishment of greenhouses and plastic farms and laboratories for tissue reproduction of varieties resistant to climate changes.

The overall goal: To increase the resilience of the agricultural sector towards climate change to protect and enhance the country’s food security, reduce soil degradation, and increase agricultural revenues to achieve economic diversification, reduce poverty, and support rural women through the use of modern technologies and technologies as part of achieving sustainable development goals.

3. Health sector:

The most important topics:

  1. Enhancing the resilience of the health sector by preventing diseases caused by climate change.

  2. Rehabilitation of health fragile and poor communities exposed to health risks and restore their resilience and adaptability to the negative effects of climate change.

  3. Raising health awareness among communities vulnerable to diseases related to climate change and supporting the Health and Family Program.

  4. Combating endemic and epidemic diseases caused by climate change.

The overall goal: To increase the resilience of the health sector to face climate change by providing the necessary and comprehensive support to health institutions and infrastructure to enhance the national response, rehabilitate health fragile communities and build their resilience and adaptation, which will contribute to achieving sustainable development goals.

4. Natural systems and forests:

The most important themes:

  1. Increasing the number of natural reserves and considering the diversity of those reserves to ensure the protection of the largest possible number of endangered species and fragile ecosystems, while activating the laws and legislations that include the realization of this protection. Activate the laws and legislations that guarantee the achievement of this protection.

  2. Preserving the ecosystems that provide environmental services and using some of them to ward off environmental risks and disasters.

  3. Genetic improvement of productive plant species to reach species with high productivity and tolerant of harsh conditions.

  4. Create oases in desert and dry ecosystems with the aim of protecting biodiversity from climate change.

  5. Filling the gaps in the laws and instructions in force to protect this vital sector.

The overarching goal: to protect and conserve ecosystems by increasing their resilience to adapt to the effects of climate change and adopting nature-based climate solutions to protect the fragile, rare and most vulnerable environments.

5. Coastal areas and sea level rise:

The most important topics:

  1. Repairing the environmental damage inflicted on the coastal areas in southern Iraq and treating the expansion of the salt tongue from the sea water towards the Shatt al-Arab and the city of Basra, and restoring the marine environment to its former state.

  2. Protecting coasts, reducing erosion and preserving ecosystems by encouraging the cultivation of plants and trees that reduce erosion.

  3. Protecting coastal natural resources and preparing coral reef rehabilitation programmes.

  4. Using modern information systems, modeling and climatic scenarios to assess the future effects of sea level rise and temperature rise and enhance the ability for strategic planning and proactive adaptation to this phenomenon and to protect the coastal strip south of Basra.

The overall objective: Develop strategies to protect the marine environment, its biodiversity, and coastal areas to reduce its erosion, increase its resilience to sea level rise, increase its ability to absorb carbon, preserve the related natural environments, and address the risks and threats to marine life and infrastructure.

6. Sewage and Waste Sector:

The most important topics:

  1. Treatment of sewage and sewage water and its recycling for use in the agricultural sector for restricted and unrestricted irrigation through a three-stage treatment, as well as for use in the industrial and energy sectors.

  2. Providing exemplary wastewater treatment plants in villages and rural communities to produce irrigation water, and mobile plants for on-site organic waste treatment to produce biogas and organic fertilizer.

  3. Supporting and assigning industrial sewage treatment plants projects to large and medium industrial establishments to provide alternative and non-conventional water sources and reduce river water pollution.

  4. Rehabilitation of the sewage sector in order to increase the resilience of the water sector and reduce the negative effects of climate change.

  5. Encouraging investment and preparing all its requirements with the involvement of the private sector in the process of proper management of solid waste, recycling and treating it by technical methods and using modern technology to benefit from it in the production of electric power and kerosene fuel to preserve natural resources.

The overall goal: To support the integrated management of waste and to promote and improve its management and recycling to produce energy from solid waste, preserve the environment, improve the quality of treated water, and reduce energy consumption as part of achieving sustainable development goals.

7. Climate, recurring and slow-onset events and risks resulting from climate change:

The most important themes:

  1. Building and developing a monitoring and early warning system and building capacities to monitor extreme and severe weather events such as droughts, floods, rain, dust and dust storms, to take the necessary preparations and reduce human and economic losses.

  2. Preparing an atlas (deaths and economic losses from extreme events due to climate change in Iraq) and establishing a network of weather stations across the country to monitor and provide meteorological and hydrological information.

  3. Providing financial and technical support for the preparation of computer technologies, remote sensing, analysis and prediction of climate fluctuations according to the scenarios of the IPCC ,as part of a proactive adaptation to reduce the greatest extent of losses and damages that may result from it, and the creation of future climate models and scenarios for Iraq for the end of the twenty-first century.

  4. Strengthening the national and regional partnership to manage crises and climate change disasters and reduce the risks associated with them.

  5. Enhance the resilience of ecosystems to acute and extreme events (floods, floods, droughts) to reduce the risks of natural disasters related to climate change.

Overarching Objective: The country aims to increase the predictive capacity of government institutions' infrastructure and improve preparedness by reducing exposure to climate risks and increasing the resilience, resilience and resilience of sectors affected by the negative impacts of climate change.

8. Higher education, scientific research, science and technology

The most important themes:

  1. Creation of scientific departments specialized in the issue of climate change in universities, colleges and related institutes.

  2. Working to harmonize academic projects and dissertations for graduate students with national needs on the subject of climate change in accordance with approved international standards.

The overall goal: Adopting scientific research and technological development as a basic foundation for success through joint work between national sectors to achieve the objectives of this document.

9. Energy sector

 The most important themes:

  1. Using a ( nature base solution) method for afforestation surrounding the power plants.

  2. Improving the efficiency of transportation and distribution and adopting modern technological methods that help to adapt to rising temperatures.

  3. Changing the specifications of electrical equipment used in the electricity sector in line with the increase in temperatures.

  4. Achieving balance and integration between the various means of transportation and making it an integrated system to maximize the volume of transportation in Iraq and to set the regulations governing that.

  • Developing and providing mass public transportation and infrastructure to avoid traffic congestion and improve living standards, including the provision of railways to transport passengers, goods and equipment between governorates and improve their efficiency.

  • Providing advanced transportation technologies that are flexible and able to withstand the negative effects of climate change.

  • Striving to develop airports, land, sea and river transport centers, and the use of modern, environmentally friendly trains, such as electric-powered trains.

  • Supporting maritime transport and its importance in linking Iraq with the world and its impact on the international trade movement and raising the standard of living for the individual.

  • Conducting comprehensive studies to assess the effects of climate changes and high temperatures on the transport sector.

The overall goal: Increasing the resilience of the energy sector in the face of climate change through support for adaptation programs and working simultaneously with mitigation axes to enhance the national response in addressing climate changes and increasing the percentage of the population benefiting from electricity and transportation services.

10. Tourism and World Heritage (Natural and Cultural)

Tourism (spatial, archaeological, religious, environmental), antiquities and heritage museums, various theaters, buildings, markets, inns, heritage houses, festivals, conferences, cinematographic works, theatrical and performing arts, plastic arts, fashion, publications, exhibitions, advertising, craft industries and literary heritage such as poetry, prose, tales, novels, stories, proverbs, music, and institutions concerned with childhood Culture and community awareness are considered an important source of national income and synonymous with fossil fuels, as they have been revitalized, as well as important archaeological sites such as historical capitals and the need to preserve and promote them and to excavate important new sites. Or because they are located near major river courses and current and future dam sites.

The most important themes:

  1. Encouraging the traditional knowledge of the local people to reduce poverty and improve the social and economic reality.

  2. Working to preserve heritage by registering archaeological, heritage and cultural sites in Iraq as World Heritage sites to protect them from the negative effects of climate change and the international community’s contribution to protecting them.

  3. Preserving the natural ecosystems from the negative effects of climate change, protecting the economic ecosystem services of the local population, and sustaining the outstanding universal values ​​of the World Heritage property.

  4. Strengthening the management plan of the marshes property of southern Iraq for the world heritage by adding a paragraph assessing the negative impacts of climate change on the outstanding universal value of this property as a world heritage.

The overall goal: To increase the resilience of the tourism and world heritage sector and encourage it as one of the most important sources of diversification of the country’s economy resources, protect archaeological sites, world heritage and indigenous peoples from the threats of climate change, and encourage sustainable eco-tourism.

Chapter Three

Mitigation measures and co-benefits:

The main objective of mitigation policies is to reduce greenhouse gas emissions by taking measures and plans to prepare a document that achieves a balance between the requirements of the Framework Convention on Climate Change and the Paris Agreement attached to it on the one hand, diversifying the sources of the economy, sustainability of exports of fossil fuels and the stability of the global market for this type of fuel from On the other hand, to ensure the achievement of the sustainable development goals until suitable alternatives are found and to limit the impact of response measures taken by developed countries on the economies of developing countries and the negative social and environmental repercussions that will cast a shadow on the national economy in the event such measures are taken.

The amount of the reduction will be determined according to the nationally appropriate mitigation measures that Iraq will submit to the agreement’s secretariat in the future in the form of plans, strategies, programs and projects, which will depend on the amount of the baseline for the usual amount of work for each project that includes a reduction in emissions and according to the main emitting sectors, targeting the main gases in the approved greenhouse gas regulations The IPCC hascarbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide.

The most important sectors through which Iraq will work to achieve the goals of its national contributions in reducing greenhouse gas emissions and diversifying the sources of its national economy are the oil, gas, electricity, industry, trade, agriculture, transportation, waste and housing sectors.

The pathways aimed at mitigating the emissions of carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases can be described as follows:

  • Wide dissemination of the culture of environmentally friendly technologies that reduce emissions to include all types of target gases, with a focus on mechanisms related to improving energy efficiency.

  • Mobilize efforts for mitigation measures to include all triggers.

  • Dissemination of knowledge and capacity-building with the support provided by the developed countries to the developing countries to ensure consistency between the parties’ states to ensure balance and justice in opportunities, taking into account the national conditions and needs of each country.

Iraq has worked from an early age to join the agreement and then sign the Paris Agreement to work on implementing projects and strategic studies to introduce clean and renewable energies, improve energy efficiency, sound environmental management of carbon and increase the area of ​​green spaces, and projects related to the introduction of liquefied petroleum gas ( LPG) fuel as a vehicle fuel have been started In addition to the traditional fuels used and other pioneering projects in these areas and for many national sectors, including working to convert the lighting of many main streets in Baghdad to work with solar energy, and developing future plans to convert some electric power plants to combined cycle stations to reduce fuel use and increase production. In addition to taking many measures and policies that would reduce greenhouse gas emissions, including:

  • Awareness of the importance of rationalizing energy consumption.

  • Spreading the culture of using LEDenergy-saving lighting on all government and commercial buildings.

  • Issuance of energy efficiency labels by the Central Agency for Standardization and Quality Control to ensure the use of environmentally friendly goods and services.

  • Tire recycling by the Ministry of Industry to achieve a reduction in emissions.

  • Issuing green building codes, which are considered a policy in establishing smart green buildings.

  • Issuance of the solar energy law.

  • Investment of associated gas in the Basra Gas Company.

In addition to projects that have been implemented in the Kurdistan Region of Iraq, such as the establishment of power plants that operate on liquefied petroleum gas ( LPG) fuel instead of heavy fuel, and the establishment of brick factories using LPG .Instead of black oil, as well as iron production plants that operate on gasoline instead of black oil, and there are many projects underway, such as the associated gas investment project for power generation, cement plants that operate with the technology of investing waste and converting it into energy, a project to invest associated gas to produce electric power, and a treatment project Sewage water and its reuse for green belt irrigation, which is implemented in cooperation with the Japanese organization JICA in Erbil governorate, not to mention afforestation, increasing forest areas and planting green belts, noting that most of the new cities that are being built in the Kurdistan Region are sustainable cities that depend on smart lighting and recycling Using sewage for irrigation to increase green spaces and many more.

International support and technology transfer are of exceptional importance in preventing the waste of associated gas, which is an important economic wealth if invested, in addition to achieving a significant reduction in greenhouse gas emissions. The International Energy Agency estimates that leaks of methane (the main component of natural gas) in Iraq could be reduced by more than 80% using existing technology. The financial value of methane emissions in Iraq is estimated at more than 600 million dollars, which represents a great economic loss. In addition to the economic benefits that the investment of this gas will bring to Iraq, reducing methane emissions protects human health by improving air quality at the local level.

The following is a description of the pathways approved by the national sectors:

1- Energy sector (oil, gas, electricity and transportation):

Through this vital sector, Iraq seeks to voluntarily reduce its emissions if security and peace are provided on the one hand, and the developed countries fulfill their obligations in terms of financing, transfer of environmentally friendly technology and capacity building on the other hand, as the highest rate of greenhouse gas emissions results from this sector, according to what was stated in a document The first national communication, as it contributes 75% of the total emissions of Iraq, therefore, taking the following measures to reduce the emissions of this sector will be feasible from a development point of view:

  • Reducing the levels of associated gas flaring and investing it in oil and natural gas extraction processes.

  • Investing in and developing the petroleum industries to reduce resource depletion and reduce emissions simultaneously, in particular improving and monitoring associated gas flaring technology to reduce methane emissions and avoid gas “venting” through good design, including through gas recovery and recycling .

  • Conducting periodic detection programs for methane gas leaks in oil and gas facilities for the purpose of repair ( LDAR) in cooperation with international partners (Global Methane Alliance- GMA) and oil and gas companies operating in Iraq.

  • The use of combined cycles to increase the production of electrical energy

  • Changing the type of liquid fuel to gaseous fuel in electric (gas) power production plants and improving the quality of the fuel used, which contributes to reducing carbon emissions.

  • Reducing emissions by adopting mechanisms to improve energy efficiency and rationalize its consumption.

  • Converting heavy fuel electric power stations to LPGand dry gas fuels, which can be provided by catching associated gas and reducing methane emissions.

  • The use of hydroelectric power, which is a clean source of energy.

  • The use of carbon trapping and storage ( CCS ) techniquesor carbon trapping and storage and use of CCUSto reduce carbon emissions and benefit from it in industrial processes.

  • Localizing renewable energy technology, especially with regard to solar energy, due to Iraq's strategic location, which is considered a promising area for the localization of this type of technology.

  • The use of advanced and sustainable public transportation and environmentally friendly technologies such as the construction of the monorail project and the gradual transition towards sustainable transportation.

  • Gradual shift towards hybrid and environmentally friendly vehicles.

  • The use of aircraft with more fuel-efficient engines and a more efficient operating system.

  • Reviewing and updating existing laws and determining the need for new laws regarding trade and industrial investment laws and customs tariffs.

  • Production of electric energy from wind in promising areas, especially in the Kurdistan region.

  • Supporting the participation of the private sector in developing the public transport system in accordance with participatory mechanisms with the public sector.

2- Industry sector:

The industrial sector, in its public, private and mixed aspects, is one of the components of the non-oil economy in Iraq, and the contribution of this sector to the gross domestic product averaged 2.4 percent during the years (2008-2011). The Ministry of Industry and Minerals seeks as it manages this sector, which is one of the sectors that contribute to the emission of greenhouse gases through a set of measures:

  1. Developing and qualifying industrial processes in existing projects, such as in the manufacture of cement, bricks, fertilizers and petrochemicals.

  2. Recycling and waste management (recycling and waste management), such as projects for recycling heat and gases emitted from industrial furnaces and recycling plastic and tires.

  3. Supporting the private industrial sector in the field of emissions reduction through the application of the mechanisms mentioned within the industrial strategy, such as small and medium enterprises, to support the development of technological paths, innovation and the transfer of environmentally friendly industrial technologies.

3- Agriculture sector

The agricultural sector is one of the important economic sectors in the country and is one of the main contributors to greenhouse gas emissions, as most of Iraq’s crops are grains such as wheat, barley, rice and corn, in addition to horticultural crops and palm trees. as follows:

  • Controlling the cultivation of crops that produce a large amount of methane gas, such as rice cultivation, as well as reducing its consumption of a large amount of water, combating soil erosion and rehabilitating its degraded lands .

  • Using alternative energy units such as solar and wind energy in the agricultural sector to operate irrigation pumps and use efficient irrigation systems.

  • Implementing integrated management to combat natural forest fires and rehabilitate burned and degraded forests. Establish a system to protect and maintain natural and artificial forests, increase their areas, and establish green belts to reduce CO2 emissions.

  • Increasing environmental awareness of climate-smart agriculture. Improving the techniques of using nitrogen fertilizers to reduce. N2O emissions and encouraging no-tillage farming to reduce emissions resulting from the use of agricultural machinery as it is an experiment successfully applied in the Kurdistan Region.

  • Reducing emissions from enteric fermentation in cattle and sheep by identifying and improving appropriate feeds for them.

4- Waste sector:

Iraq seeks to localize ways of green investment in waste management of all kinds to diversify its economy sources through the following procedures:

  1. Issuance of the Solid Waste Management Law, which encourages waste recycling, converting waste into energy, and eliminating waste burning.

  2. Using plasma technology to produce electrical energy from waste.

  3. Implementation of an integrated waste management system.

  4. Investing methane from landfill sites in the production of electric power.

  5. Tire recycling project.

  6. Energy production through waste treatment to generate electricity and for heating, especially in modern cities adjacent to sewage treatment plants.

5- The housing sector:

Through the housing sector, Iraq seeks to implement the concepts of rationalizing the consumption of electric energy and moving towards low-emissions paths, depending on the localization of principles to stimulate the use of renewable energy in Iraq, as follows:

  1. Using energy-saving lighting technologies.

  2. The use of thermal insulation techniques.

  3. Issuing green building codes and modern technologies for thermal insulation, lighting and sustainable cities.

  4. Using the effective design of the buildings to make the most of the lighting and solar energy.

  5. Implementation of photovoltaic technologies for electricity distributed in small regions and cities.

  6. An integrated design that includes the use of smart meter technology inside buildings.

  7. The trend towards integrated solar photovoltaic energy in buildings.

  8. Enhancing the production and use of building materials and environmentally friendly local products.

  9. Using modern construction techniques such as GRCand ICF ,which is represented in reducing the amount of reinforcing steel and cement used in construction

Monitoring, Reporting and Verification:

The monitoring, reporting and verification system is considered one of the most important requirements necessary to build a transparent system that reflects our needs according to a solid methodology through which we can draw clear paths to describe the necessary legislation, laws, and instructions to mitigate emissions and the repercussions of this on adaptation. Emissions in measured quantities that can be reported with the availability of numerical measurements based on a legislative ground that includes all sectors.

Through the support mechanisms in the Framework Convention on Climate Change and the Paris Agreement, and in cooperation with the United Nations Development Program ( UNDP ), Iraq seeksto obtain the necessary support to design the required monitoring form according to each sector, provided that it includes all forms of activities and for all sectors in a flexible and scalable manner, as Iraq realizes that these systems It will improve the transparency framework in the following:

  • National inventory and reporting of greenhouse gases for each emitting activity.

  • Incentivizing oil and gas companies operating in Iraq to report methane emissions within known transparent frameworks, such as the Oil, Gas and Methane Partnership Framework (OGMP).

  • Collaborate on campaigns to directly measure methane from oil and gas facilities and use satellite data to establish a baseline for emissions.

  • Assessment of progress in implementing the Nationally Determined Contribution.

  • Identifying and assessing the adverse effects of climate change on fragile sectors.

  • Describe the co-benefits between mitigation and adaptation.

  • The effect of implementing response measures on the national economy.

  • Assessment of national needs and the type of climate technology that contributes to achieving sustainable development goals.

  • Determine the type of capacity that needs to be built to ensure feasible national mitigation measures in creating green jobs.

  • Determining the type of financing and investment that guarantees the implementation of the national contribution in accordance with the country's national policy.

  • Localize technologies and software that have the ability to design predictive models for mitigation and adaptation

  • Describe the features of verification of actions taken to mitigate and adapt to the effects of climate change.

  • Determining the type, shape and level of institutional structures required in accordance with international and national trends to confront climate change.

Iraq is determined to advance the climate change file because it realizes its danger at all levels while it seeks to establish the concepts of a green economy. However, the National Center for Climate Change, which represents the institutional structure concerned with managing climate changes nationally, still needs more support and backing, in order to Building and managing an integrated and transparent system for measurement, reporting and verification in particular, and advancing the reality of the climate change file in general, as international financial, technical and diplomatic support must be provided to upgrade the type of climate capabilities and skills to enable it to participate in international, regional and national policy-making for the participation of the international community in its actions to reduce the phenomenon Climate change.
